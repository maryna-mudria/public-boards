Lifecycle and fixture-ready rules

Fixture-ready: a Client card is fixture-ready if and only if it is done, or it is in testing with all its own Client-only acceptance evidence recorded, every listed Client dependency itself fixture-ready, and its only remaining incomplete criteria or blockers are limited to its own listed external server gates plus waiting for those fixture-ready Client dependencies to become done. No other blocker qualifies. Because fixture-ready is recursive, the Client dependency graph must be acyclic. Moving a card to testing does not make it fixture-ready by itself. Fixture-ready permits downstream fixture implementation and testing only. It never permits a dependent card to become done. Any card with Client dependencies may move to done only after every listed Client dependency is itself done, in addition to satisfying its own acceptance criteria and external gate rules. Evidence obtained against fixtures must be labeled fixture-only. Fixture-only evidence cannot satisfy or be described as live or release evidence, and cannot close an external gate.
